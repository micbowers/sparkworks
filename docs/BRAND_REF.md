## PROGRAM IDENTITY

**Name:** Sparkworks (only name parents/students ever see)
**One-liner:** "An 8-session program that teaches kids to think through hard problems — through games, not lectures."
**Tagline:** Think through anything.
**Stage:** Founders Cohort / Proof of Concept — pre-revenue, pre-live-test. No scale projections in any external materials.
**Cohort name:** Founding Sparks (use with pride, not apology)

**Two tracks — always market separately, never as undifferentiated "grades 2-6":**
- Ember Track — grades 2-3
- Blaze Track — grades 4-6 (primary marketing focus for Founding Sparks)

**Structure:** 8 sessions over 4 weeks (1–2x/week). Each track: max 6 students, dedicated instructor.
**Pricing:** Founding Sparks rate $149 / all 8 sessions (pilot, completed). Season 1 — Fall 2026 is $449 / all 8 sessions, starting the week of September 7, 2026.

## COLOR PALETTE

The Sparkworks palette is **10 colors, all in the same tonal family** — section accents anchored on Ember's saturation and lightness range. Same studio, different hues.

| Name | Hex | Role | Restriction |
|------|-----|------|-------------|

| Spark Blue | `#1A5C82` | Primary identity — wordmark hero backgrounds, footer, primary CTAs | Use for all dark hero surfaces. |

### Section accents — sprint colors + brand accent

Each color identifies one sprint section AND has a secondary role in the broader system. Ember and Live Teal do double duty (brand accent + section).

### Specials — used sparingly

| Name | Hex | Role | Restriction |
|------|-----|------|-------------|
| Spark Red | `#CF2C38` | Capstone events (Spark Challenge), urgency tags, alert states, error states | One use per page max. Never confuse with Ember. |
| Spark Yellow | `#F5C400` | Da Vinci Badge, earned moments, celebration | Keep it rare. |

### Neutrals

| Name | Hex | Role | Restriction |
|------|-----|------|-------------|
| Bone White | `#F2EFE8` | Accent surfaces — callouts, table rows, secondary panels | **Never as main background. Never as a text color.** |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Default page background, card surfaces, light text on dark | Default. Use everywhere unless an accent surface is needed. |

### Color usage rules

- **Default body and headings:** Steel Black on White
- **Hero surfaces:** Spark Blue background; the largest/highest-level header gets white at 100%; all smaller text gets 85%
- **Light text on dark — opacity ladder:**
  - **100% white** — reserved for the largest header on the dark surface. Often the SPARKWORKS wordmark, but can also be a hero H1 like "Find The Alien." or "The Spark Challenge." There is one 100% element per dark surface, max.
  - **85% white** — default for everything else: subheadings, body, taglines, kickers, supporting lines
  - **60% white** — de-emphasized text only: footnotes, metadata, captions
- **Section identity:** Each sprint section uses its assigned color for kickers, dot patterns, callout borders, badge accents
- **Ember and Live Teal pull double duty** (brand accent + section color). This is intentional — the relaxed rationing reflects how often they appear in real materials.

## SESSION ARCHITECTURE

**4-phase format (every session — 60 min total, phase timings are flexible):**

| Phase | Name | Time | Rule |
|-------|------|------|------|
| 1 | IGNITE | 8-10 min | High-energy warm-up. Do not shorten below 8 min. |
| 2 | SHARPEN | 8-10 min | One concept only. Case study. AHA moment. |
| 3 | ENGAGE | 30-40 min | The game. Instructor circulates, does not solve. |
| 4 | REINFORCE | 5-10 min | Apply, reflect, real world. Always end with closing question. |

Phase ranges are guidance — instructors flex within them based on how the session is going. **Sessions stay 60 minutes total.** If Engage runs long, Reinforce gets shorter. If Sharpen lands fast, Engage gets longer. Reinforce never gets cut entirely — the closing question is non-negotiable.

**The closing question (non-negotiable):** "Before you go — what did you notice about your own thinking today?"

**8-session sequence:**

| # | Section | Lesson | Game | Skill |
|---|---------|--------|------|-------|
| 1 | See What Others Miss | Pattern Detection | Knight's Tour 5×5 | Knowing when your pattern is wrong |
| 2 | See What Others Miss | Elimination | Logic Grid + Reverse Deduction | Elimination is the fastest path to the answer |
| 3 | Understand the System | Constraints | River Crossing + Boat Formula | Constraints point toward the solution |
| 4 | Understand the System | Hidden Rules | Rule Hunter + Mastermind | Hidden rules are where the advantage lives |
| 5 | Decide Without All the Facts | Estimation | Fermi Estimation Stations | Close enough to make a good decision |
| 6 | Decide Without All the Facts | Order of Operations | Poker Card Formula + Spot the AI Mistake | Understanding rules deeper than your opponent |
| 7 | Think Beyond What You Control | Strategy | Connect Four Tournament | Integration of every skill under pressure |
| 8 | Think Beyond What You Control | Game Theory | Connect Four + Game Theory Problems | Thinking several moves ahead |

**Real-world case studies (one closes each session):**
- S1: Titanic iceberg warnings (1912)
- S2: Dr. John Snow, cholera epidemic 1854
- S3: Apollo 13
- S5: Enrico Fermi, nuclear test estimation
- S7/S8: Strategic thinkers (Sun Tzu, game theory)

**Differentiator 3 headline (use consistently):** "Every Session Ends in the Real World"

## AUDIENCE — INVESTED PARENT PROFILE

Believes development is worth active investment. Anxious about AI's impact on their child's future. Evaluates based on reasoning quality, not entertainment value. Reads descriptions carefully. Asks "what will my kid actually do?" Talks to other parents before buying.

**Unspoken questions to answer proactively:**
- Is this just passive learning? → 30-40 min of active problem-solving each session, no lectures
- Is this worth the schedule slot? → 8 sessions, you'll know by session 3
- What will my kid do differently? → specific skills: estimate, eliminate, catch AI mistakes
- How is this different? → we teach method, not subject
- Is this proven? → Founding Sparks, deliberate first run, founding pricing

## WHAT NOT TO DO — HARD RULES

- Never market Ember and Blaze tracks as one undifferentiated "grades 2-6" or "ages 8-12" program
- Never reference scale projections or franchise thinking in external materials
- Never suggest parents present at Session 8 — parent presentations were permanently removed
- Never use "Logix Forge" — retired
- Never use "Leonardo Di Vinci Badge" — correct name is "Da Vinci Badge"
- Never use "Execute" as a phase name — correct name is "Reinforce"
- Never use Bone White (#F2EFE8) as the main page background — White (#FFFFFF) is the default background for all materials. Bone White is reserved for accent surfaces only: callouts, table rows, secondary panels.
- Never use Bone White (#F2EFE8) as a text color — it is too low-contrast. Use White (#FFFFFF) when light text on a dark background is needed.
- Never use white at 100% on dark backgrounds except for the largest/highest-level header on that surface (the wordmark, or a hero H1). All other white text on dark is 85%. Only one 100% element per dark surface.
- Never confuse Spark Red (#CF2C38) with Ember Orange (#D4501A) — they have separate roles. Ember is the brand accent (wordmark, kickers, primary CTAs). Spark Red is for capstones, alerts, and urgency only.
- Never describe the program as "proven" or "tested" — it is "carefully designed and structured"
